Basic Calculator

Facts:

Invention: The first mechanical calculator was invented by French mathematician Blaise
Pascal in the 17th century. It was called the Pascaline and could perform addition and subtraction.

Electronics: The first electronic calculator was created in the 1960s. It was much larger than modern calculators and used vacuum tubes.

Scientific Calculators: Scientific calculators, capable of performing complex mathematical functions like trigonometry and logarithms, became widely available in the 1970s and 1980s.

Graphing Calculators: Graphing calculators, which can plot graphs of equations, were introduced in the 1980s. They are commonly used in mathematics and science education.

Solar-Powered Calculators: Many calculators are powered by solar cells, which convert light into electricity. This makes them environmentally friendly and eliminates the need for batteries.

Calculator Watches: In the 1980s, calculator watches gained popularity. These wristwatches featured a miniature calculator keypad and display, allowing users to perform calculations on their wrists.
